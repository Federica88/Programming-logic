Calypso

Calypso, daughter of Atlas, was a nymph in Greek Mythology, who lived on the island of Ogygia.

Circe

Circe was an enchantress who was skilled in the magic of transmutation,illusion and necromancy

Lotus Eaters

Lotus Eaters in Greek Mythology were a race of people living on an island dominated by lotus plants. The lotus flowers were the primary food for them and they were a narcotic

Polyphemus

A cyclops in Greek Mythology is a member of a primordial race of giants, each with a single eye in the center of the forehead. The word cyclops means "round-eyed" or "circle-eyed".

Scylla-Charybdis

Scylla and Charybdis were mythical sea monsters. They were sited on opposite sides of the Strait of Messina between Sicily and the Italian mainland. Being between Scylla and Charybdis is an idiom still used today by people and its meaning is "having to choose between two evils.

Sirens

In Greek Mythology, the Sirens were dangerous creatures. They used to enchant sailors by singing because they wanted them to shipwreck on the coast of their island. Roman poets placed them on some small island called scopuli.
